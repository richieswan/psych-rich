⚡ Sympathetic vs Parasympathetic

These two branches of the autonomic nervous system work in opposition to regulate internal balance (homeostasis).

  • Sympathetic (Fight-or-Flight): Increases heart rate, dilates pupils, inhibits digestion, releases adrenaline, redirects blood to muscles.
  • Parasympathetic (Rest-and-Digest): Decreases heart rate, constricts pupils, stimulates digestion, promotes recovery and calming.
💡 Remember: Sympathetic = Stress response. Parasympathetic = Peace. Think "P" for Peace, "S" for Stress.

🔬 Neurons & Neurotransmitters

  • Neurons: Specialised cells that transmit nerve impulses. Three types: sensory, motor, interneurons.
  • Synapse: The gap between two neurons. Neurotransmitters carry signals across it.
  • Key Neurotransmitters:
    • GABA — inhibitory; reduces neural activity; involved in relaxation
    • Glutamate — excitatory; key role in memory & learning
    • Dopamine — reward, motivation, movement
    • Serotonin — mood, sleep, wellbeing

😓 What is Stress?

Stress is a psychological and physiological response to perceived demands that exceed available resources (Lazarus & Folkman, 1984).

  • Eustress: Positive stress — motivating and short-term (e.g., before a performance)
  • Distress: Negative stress — chronic or overwhelming
  • Acute stress: Short-term, intense (e.g., near-miss accident)
  • Chronic stress: Long-term, ongoing (e.g., financial pressure)

🔄 The Stress Response (HPA Axis)

When a stressor is perceived, the Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal (HPA) axis is activated:

  • 1. Hypothalamus detects threat → releases CRH
  • 2. Pituitary gland releases ACTH into bloodstream
  • 3. Adrenal glands release cortisol → sustains stress response
🔬 Cortisol helps mobilise energy but suppresses immune function during prolonged stress — explaining why chronic stress leads to illness.

🛡️ Coping Strategies

  • Approach coping: Directly addressing the stressor (e.g., studying for an exam)
  • Avoidance coping: Avoiding the stressor (e.g., procrastinating) — effective short-term but maladaptive long-term
  • Problem-focused coping: Taking action to change the situation
  • Emotion-focused coping: Managing feelings about the situation

🌿 Wellbeing

Wellbeing is a multi-dimensional concept involving:

  • Physical wellbeing: Body health, sleep, exercise, nutrition
  • Psychological wellbeing: Emotional regulation, mental health, self-efficacy
  • Social wellbeing: Relationships, belonging, community connection
📌 For VCE, know the difference between internal and external protective factors for mental wellbeing.

📊 Lazarus & Folkman — Transactional Model of Stress

Stress is a transaction between a person and their environment, involving two appraisal stages:

  • Primary appraisal: "Is this a threat?" — Irrelevant / Benign-positive / Stressful
  • Secondary appraisal: "Can I cope?" — Assessing resources available

If demands exceed resources → stress is experienced. This model explains why the same event causes different stress levels in different people.

🔁 Selye's General Adaptation Syndrome (GAS)

Selye proposed a 3-stage physiological response to prolonged stress:

  • Stage 1 — Alarm: Initial fight-or-flight response activated (SNS)
  • Stage 2 — Resistance: Body adapts; appears to cope but is depleted
  • Stage 3 — Exhaustion: Resources depleted; illness, burnout, breakdown
💡 GAS is a biological model; Lazarus & Folkman is a cognitive/psychological model. Know the difference!

Exam Tips

Smart strategies to maximise your marks

🎯

Command Words Matter

  • Define — give the exact meaning, no elaboration needed
  • Describe — give characteristics, no explanation needed
  • Explain — give why/how with reasoning
  • Evaluate — give strengths AND limitations
  • Compare — state similarities AND differences
🧬

Nervous System Questions

  • Always specify which branch of the NS (SNS vs PNS vs ANS)
  • Name the neurotransmitter AND its effect
  • Use directional language: "increases", "inhibits", "activates"
  • Link structure to function (e.g., sympathetic → dilates pupils → to improve vision in threat)
😰

Stress & Wellbeing Questions

  • Name the model you're using (Lazarus & Folkman / Selye GAS)
  • Apply to the scenario — don't just define
  • For GAS: name the stage, describe physiological response
  • Distinguish approach vs avoidance coping clearly
✍️

Writing Extended Responses

  • Open with a direct statement — don't re-quote the question
  • Use psychological terminology throughout
  • Structure: Define → Explain → Apply → Evaluate (if needed)
  • Finish by connecting back to the question stem
📅

Study Strategy

  • Use spaced repetition for key terms — review after 1, 3, 7, 14 days
  • Practice past SAC questions under timed conditions
  • Draw the NS hierarchy diagram from memory daily
  • Teach someone else — it reveals gaps in your knowledge
⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing the somatic and autonomic nervous systems
  • Forgetting secondary appraisal in Lazarus & Folkman
  • Using "adrenaline" when asked about cortisol (different pathways!)
  • Describing instead of explaining when "explain" is asked
