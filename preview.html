Research Methods

Experimental research designs test cause-and-effect relationships by manipulating an independent variable and measuring the effect on a dependent variable.

  • Between-groups design: different participants in each condition.
  • Within-groups design: same participants experience all conditions.
  • Mixed design: combines both.

Gut–Brain Axis

The gut–brain axis refers to the bidirectional communication network between the gastrointestinal system and the brain.

Gut microbiota can influence:

  • Mood
  • Stress responses
  • Cognition
  • Immune functioning

Sleep

Sleep is regulated by two biological mechanisms:

  • Circadian rhythms
  • Sleep-wake homeostasis

Key Terms

Independent Variable – Variable manipulated by researcher

Dependent Variable – Variable measured in the experiment

Control Group – Participants who do not receive treatment

Experimental Group – Participants who receive treatment

Gut Microbiota – Microorganisms living in the digestive tract

Circadian Rhythm – Biological processes occurring in ~24 hour cycles

Exam Tips

  • Always define key terms clearly.
  • Use correct psychological terminology.
  • For evaluation questions give a strength and limitation.
  • Link answers directly to the scenario.
  • Use examples when possible.
